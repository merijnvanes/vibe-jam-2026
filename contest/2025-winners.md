# Vibe Jam 2025 — Winners & Takeaways

> Sources:
> - <https://x.com/levelsio/status/...> (winner announcement)
> - <https://x.com/karpathy/status/...> (Karpathy reply, Apr 24 2025)

## Judges

@karpathy, @timsoret, @mrdoob, @s13k_, @levelsio

1,000+ games submitted.

## Winners

### #1 — The Great Taxi Assignment ($10,000)

By @TomasBencko — <https://great-taxi-assignment.netlify.app>

GTA-like taxi driver simulator.

Jury highlights:
- "Funny, cute, well executed, cool music"
- "Fun game — single player GTA style"
- "Crazy taxi, cool vibes, great car physics, feels good, instant fun"

Criticism: "Level design a bit flat", "A little bit too easy"

### #2 — Vibeware ($5,000)

By @mattgordonxyz — <https://vibeware.vercel.app>

You play as a bot given tasks. Original concept.

Jury highlights:
- "Super fun concept, original idea, relatively polished"

Criticism: "Fun concept, but janky"

### #3 — Vector Tango ($2,500)

By @scobel — <https://vector-tango.com>

Air traffic control simulator.

Jury highlights:
- "Looks pretty clean/polished"

Criticism: "Elevation control (A/Z) is inconvenient"

## What Karpathy Looked For

> "I was looking for games that had **polish**, were **unique/surprising**, **technically impressive**, and of course — **fun**."

> "This is the kernel of the future. Boundless human creativity, details handed off, visiting each other's worlds, vibing."

## Our Take

The winners set a **vibe** more than the other submissions. Technical complexity was not the main differentiator — it was feel, personality, and instant fun.
